Tue, January 30, 2018 • What does visual cortex do?

Wandell, B. A., Dumoulin, S. O., & Brewer, A. A. (2007). Visual field maps in human cortex. Neuron, 56(2), 366–383. Retrieved from http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2007.10.012

Optional

Hubel, D. H. (1982). Exploration of the primary visual cortex, 1955-78. Nature, 299(5883), 515–524. http://neurosciences.us/courses/systems/CentralVision/hubel82.pdf.

Hubel, D. H., & Wiesel, T. N. (1962). Receptive fields, binocular interaction and functional architecture in the cat’s visual cortex. The Journal of Physiology, 160(1), 106–154. https://doi.org/10.1113/jphysiol.1962.sp006837

Palmer, S. E. (1999). Vision Science: Photons to Phenomenology. MIT Press.

Assignment details

Discuss:

What are visual field maps? How are they measured or acquired? How does this method compare with single unit recordings? What do visual field maps tell us about the function of visual cortex?

As a reminder, your writing assignment document should be: - 1 full page, single-spaced - size 11 font, reasonable borders (<=1 inch) - without your name on the document or in filename - upload your text via Turnitin

Thu, February 1, 2018 • Face processing

Kanwisher, N., McDermott, J., & Chun, M. M. (1997). The fusiform face area: a module in human extrastriate cortex specialized for face perception. The Journal of Neuroscience: The official journal of the Society for Neuroscience, 17(11), 4302–4311. Retrieved from https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/9151747

Optional

Behrmann, M., & Plaut, D. C. (2013). Distributed circuits, not circumscribed centers, mediate visual recognition. Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 17(5), 210–219. Retrieved from http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2013.03.007

Perrett, D. I., Rolls, E. T., & Caan, W. (1982). Visual neurones responsive to faces in the monkey temporal cortex. Experimental Brain Research, 47(3), 329–342. https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00239352

Assignment details

Discuss:

What do Kanwisher et al. mean when they say that fusiform cortex is i) a module that is ii) specialized for face perception? What is an alternative view?

Tue, February 6, 2018 • Perception vs. action

Goodale, M. A., Milner, A. D., Jakobson, L. S., & Carey, D. P. (1991). A neurological dissociation between perceiving objects and grasping them. Nature, 349(6305), 154–156. https://doi.org/10.1038/349154a0

Optional

Goodale, M. A., & Milner, A. D. (1992). Separate visual pathways for perception and action. Trends in Neurosciences, 15(1), 20–25. https://doi.org/10.1016/0166-2236(92)90344-8

Assignment details

Discuss:

How do Goodale et al. distinguish between “perceiving objects” and the perceptual processing required to grasp them? Is this evidence for separable processing streams devoted to “perception” and “action”?

Tue, February 8, 2017 • “Reading” the mind via fMRI

Kay, K. N., Naselaris, T., Prenger, R. J., & Gallant, J. L. (2008). Identifying natural images from human brain activity. Nature, 452(7185), 352–355. https://doi.org/10.1038/nature06713. Supplemental materials.

Optional

Kay, K. N., & Gallant, J. L. (2009). I can see what you see. Nature Neuroscience, 12(3), 245–245. https://doi.org/10.1038/nn0309-245

DeYoe, E. A., Bandettini, P., Neitz, J., Miller, D., & Winans, P. (1994). Functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) of the human brain. Journal of Neuroscience Methods, 54(2), 171–187. http://doi.org/10.1016/0165-0270(94)90191-0

Assignment details

Discuss:

Why did the popular press reports of this work describe it as “mind-reading”? Is it?

Tues, February 13, 2018 • Does neuroscience need behavior?

Krakauer, J. W., Ghazanfar, A. A., Gomez-Marin, A., MacIver, M. A., & Poeppel, D. (2017). Neuroscience needs behavior: Correcting a reductionist bias. Neuron, 93(3), 480–490. Retrieved from http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.neuron.2016.12.041

Optional

Jonas, E., & Kording, K. P. (2017). Could a neuroscientist understand a microprocessor? PLoS Computational Biology, 13(1), e1005268. Public Library of Science. Retrieved January 3, 2018, from http://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article/file?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1005268&type=printable

Assignment details

Discuss:

Does neuroscience need behavior? Why or why not?
